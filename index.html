About The Project

Revolutionizing High-Rise Building Maintenance

Our Wall Climbing Glass Cleaning Robot is designed to autonomously clean the glass surfaces of buildings using vacuum suction technology, eliminating the need for dangerous human window cleaning at heights.

Developed by students of The Delhi Public School for the Causa Scientia exhibition, this project demonstrates how robotics can solve real-world safety challenges in building maintenance.

This innovation provides a safe, efficient, and cost-effective solution for glass cleaning without human risk.

Key Components

The building blocks of our innovative glass cleaning robot

ESP32 Microcontroller

The brain of our robot that controls all operations and processes sensor data.

BLDC Motor 2200kV

High-speed brushless motor that powers the vacuum suction system.

5200mAh 3S Battery

High-capacity lithium polymer battery providing long operation time.

3D Printed Chassis

Lightweight and durable frame custom designed for our robot.

Electronic Speed Controller

Controls the speed and direction of the BLDC motor precisely.

N20 12V DC Motors

Compact high-torque motors for wheel movement and navigation.

How It Works

1

Vacuum Attachment

The BLDC motor powers a propeller to create strong vacuum suction, allowing the robot to adhere securely to glass surfaces.

2

Movement

N20 DC motors drive the wheels, moving the robot across the glass surface while maintaining suction.

3

Cleaning

Microfiber cleaning pads remove dirt and grime as the robot moves, with optional water spray for tough stains.

4

Control

The ESP32 microcontroller processes commands from the remote control and manages all robot functions.
